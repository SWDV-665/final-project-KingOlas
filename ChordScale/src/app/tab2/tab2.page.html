Info / How-To Info / How-To How To Use ChordScale: Choose Root of Chord
The "Root" of a chord is the lowest fundamental note of the collection of notes that make up a particular chord. Of the 3-4 notes that make up most scales, this one will be clearly visible by the chord symbol and is one of the most important notes for inferring the context of the chord being analyzed.

Choose Type of Chord
The Chord's "Type" refers to the quality of the Chord. The relationships between each note of the chord alters how the Chord sounds and functions. Using this calculator, one will be able to decipher which notes and scales are usable when playing and improvising over the analyzed Chord.

Read Notes and Play Audio
Once the Chord Data has loaded, Click the different audio samples to hear what these scales and notes sound like. Always remember that NO NOTE IS INHERENTLY WRONG - but there are ones that sound better than others! The idea behind this app is building *Synchronicity* between your brain, your ears, and your heart. Being able to hear the notes and scales and play them back will strengthen one's musical ear, and their confidence in playing and creating music. Spend enough time with all of these sounds, and one can become fluent in music! Knowledge + Time = Wisdom!
